Karnak Temple - Luxor

A temple (from the Latin templum) is a structure usually built for the purpose of, and always dedicated to, religious or spiritual activities including prayer, sacrifice . The templum was a sacred precinct defined by a priest (or augur) as the dwelling place of a god or gods. The structure built at such a site was created to honor the Deus Loci of a certain place. Early temples were constructed on sites which the people felt had a numinous quality to them which indicated the presence of a god, gods, or spirits.


Abu Simbel - Aswan

The Great Temple at Abu Simbel was built by Ramesses II and completed around 1264 BCE. It stands 98 feet (30 metres) high and 115 feet (35 metres) long with four seated colossi flanking the entrance, two to each side, depicting Ramesses II seated on a throne and wearing the double crown of Upper and Lower Egypt.


Hatshepsut Temple - Luxor

Located at Deir el-Bahari,this mortuary temple is unique for its three-terraced built into the limestone cliffs.It was designed to commemorate the reign of Queen Hatshepsut, one of Egypt's most successful female pharaohs. Among the duties of any Egyptian monarch was the construction of monumental building projects to honor the gods and preserve the memory of their reigns for eternity.


Edfu Temple

This is the most well-preserved temple in Egypt.Dedicated to the falcon god Horus, its massive entrance pylon and intact roof provide a perfect example of ancient temples looked like in prime.The Temple of Edfu is the second largest temple in Egypt.It is known as the Temple of Horus (the falcon-headed God) and it is the most beautiful and well-preserved of all the Egyptian temples. Located between the Egyptian cities Luxor and Aswan, a French archaeologist by the name of Auguste Mariette uncovered it from its sand burial in the 1860s.


Dendera Temple - Qena

Dedicated to the goddess Hathor, this temple is famous for its vibrant colors and intricate ceiling reliefs. Hathor was one of the most prominent ancient Egyptian deities, worshipped extensively across various eras. The city of Dendera, located in the Qena Governorate, served as the primary center for her cult. Her temple in this city is considered one of the best-preserved ancient Egyptian temples. Moreover, the temple's facade is regarded as one of the most magnificent, featuring six columns topped with capitals shaped in the likeness of the goddess Hathor.


Philae Temple - Aswan

Dedicated to the goddess Isis, this temple is often called the "Pearl of Egypt." It is situated on Agilkia Island and features a blend of Ancient Egyptian and Greco-Roman architectural styles.The tiny island of Philae, a mere 450 metres long and less than 150 metres wide, captured the imagination of countless travellers to Egypt from early times. It was famed for its beauty and was known as the "Pearl of Egypt'. Plants and palm trees grew from the fertile deposits that had collected in the crevices of the granite bedrock.
